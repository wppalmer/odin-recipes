Chris Traeger Turkey Burger

Description

This was a recipe that I got from Andrew Rea's book Binging with Babish which is full of recipes from his YouTube channel of the same name. This one had grabbed my attention because I've never had a turkey burger that I deemed fantastic, and since in the episode of Parks and Rec that features this burger, shows that it is a really great burger. I can confirm that this is indeed the best turkey burger I've ever made, but it will never beat my beloved smash burger made from beef... anyways please enjoy.

Ingredients

Turkey Burger
  • 1 small eggplant
  • 1 tsp achovy paste
  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp Marmite
  • 1 1/2 lbs ground turkey
  • 1 Tbsp vegetable oil
  • 4 brioche buns
  • Kosher salt
  • Extra-virgin olive oil for drizzling
  • Microgreens
  • Papaya chutney (see below)
  • Black truffle aioli (see below)
  • Cheese crisp (see below)
Papaya Chutney
  • 1 medium papaya, peeled, halved, and seeded
  • 1/3 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1/3 cup golden raisins
  • Pinch of saffron threads
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
Black Truffle Aioli
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
  • 1 cup canola oil
  • 1/4 cup black truffle oil
Cheese Crisps
  • 6 oz Fontina cheese, shredded
  • 6 oz Parmesan cheese, grated

Directions

Turkey Burger
  1. Preheat oven to 400 F
  2. Cut the top off the eggplant, halve lengthwise, and score crosshatches into the flesh. Transfer to a rimmed baking sheet, drizzle with olive oil, and place the eggplant cut-side down. Bake for 30 to 40 minutes, until golden brown and soft. Set aside until cool enough to handle, then scoop the eggplant flesh into a large bowl.
  3. Add the anchovy paste, soy sauce, and Marmite and whisk until well combined. Add the turkey and gently fold together using a rubber spatula until well incoporated. Form the turkey mixture into four patties, pressing a divot into the center of each to prevent the burgers from puffing up during cooking. Season the patties generously with salt and pepper.
  4. In a large nonstick skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the patties to the skillet and sear on both sides until the internal temp registers 165 F, 5 to 7 minutes total.
  5. Toast the brioche buns. Dress the bottom buns with the chutney and top with the microgreens. Place the turkey patties on the microgreens and top with the cheese crisps. Dress the top buns with the aioli, then close the burgers and serve.
Papaya Chutney
  • Cut the papaya into 1/2 inch pieces. Transfer the papaya to a small saucepan and add the vinegar, raisins, and saffron. Bring to a simmer and cook for about 20 minutes, until thickened. Season with salt and pepper. Remove from heat and let cool.
Black Truffle Aioli
  • Combine the egg yolks and lemon juice in an immersion blender cup. Combine the canola oil and truffle oil in a liquid measuring cup and , with the blender running on high, slowly stream the oil mixture down the side of the blender cup. Blend until the mxutre is emulsified and has thinked to the consistency of a loose mayonnaise. Transfer to a bowl, cover, and refridgerate until ready to use or overnight.
Cheese Crisps
  1. Preheat the oven to 400 F. Line a baking sheet with a silicone baking mat or other non-stick pan liner.
  2. Combine the two cheeses in a small bowl. Arrange the cheese mixture in four even piles on the prepared baking sheet. Bake until golden brown and set. Remove the cheese crisps from the pan with a thin spatula and let cool on a wire rack.
